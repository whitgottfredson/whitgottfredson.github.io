I've logged more than 30 flight hours in a Cessna 172 — learning everything from basic maneuvers and navigation to emergency procedures and cross-country planning. I hold a USPA A-License, earned after completing 25 jumps, all 8 levels of AFF (Accelerated Freefall) training, extensive ground school, and a parachute packing certification course with accompanying USPA quizzes. From the first terrifying exit at 14,000 feet to confidently pulling my own pilot chute — skydiving has fundamentally changed how I think about risk, decision-making, and being present.

The Journey

Key Milestones

Both disciplines demand the same things: preparation, precision, and the willingness to commit. Here's a look at the major checkpoints along the way.

  1. Flying Milestones
    • First flight lesson — October 2025
    • Completed first solo ground training and pre-solo written exam
    • Logged 10 hours of dual instruction (student + instructor)
    • First solo flight in Cessna 172 N2050E — February 27, 2026
    • 30+ total flight hours logged under Part 61
    • Working toward Private Pilot Certificate
  2. Skydiving Milestones
    • Ground school and harness training completed
    • AFF Level 1 — first tandem-style freefall with two instructors
    • AFF Levels 2–4 — solo exits with instructor oversight
    • AFF Levels 5–8 — advanced maneuvers, turns, and dock jumps
    • Parachute packing course and USPA certification quiz passed
    • Jump 25 — USPA A-License earned
  3. What I've Learned
    • Both disciplines require mental discipline more than physical skill
    • Checklists and procedures exist for a reason — discipline saves lives
    • Fear is information, not a stop sign
    • The debrief is where real learning happens
